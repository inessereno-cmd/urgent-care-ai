Built Different

Maya listens to natural language, holds context across a multi-turn conversation, and responds the way a trained human would — not the way a phone tree does.

Transfer Logic

Five clear rules for when to route to a human

Maya never holds on to a call it shouldn't. These triggers are always active — no edge case gets stuck in AI limbo.

Trigger 01

Outside knowledge base

Question can't be answered from the KB — Maya acknowledges and routes rather than guessing.

Trigger 02

Caller requests a human

Any variant of "talk to someone" is honored immediately — no pushback, no retry.

Trigger 03

Emergency language detected

Chest pain, difficulty breathing, overdose — redirected to 911 in under 10 seconds.

Trigger 04

Caller frustration escalates

Repeat questions or raised tone trigger a proactive human offer — before the caller has to ask.

Trigger 05

Real-time clinical input needed

Ambiguous symptom questions that require triage judgment go straight to clinical staff.

🤝

Every transfer is a warm handoff

Maya never drops a call. On transfer, she briefs the front desk on the caller's topic — so the patient doesn't have to repeat themselves. If the front desk is unavailable, Maya offers a callback or voicemail and stays on the line.

"Let me connect you with our front desk team. I'll let them know you have a question about your insurance so you don't have to start over."
🚨 Emergency Protocol

When seconds matter, Maya doesn't hesitate

The moment Maya detects emergency language — chest pain, difficulty breathing, uncontrolled bleeding, stroke symptoms — she stops everything and redirects clearly and immediately. No screening. No delay.

Maya's exact response
"What you're describing sounds like it may be a medical emergency. Please hang up and call 911 immediately, or have someone take you to the nearest emergency room. Do not wait. Is there anything I can do to help you right now?"
Trigger to redirect: under 10 seconds
